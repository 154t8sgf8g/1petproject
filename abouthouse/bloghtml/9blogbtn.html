The bottom line is the outlook for solar technology

While on Earth the maximum efficiency of solar panels is hindered by air, which to a certain extent scatters the Sun's radiation, there is no such problem in space.
Scientists are developing projects of giant orbital satellites with solar batteries that will work 24 hours a day. From them the energy will be transmitted to ground receiving devices.
But this is a matter of the future, and for the already existing batteries efforts are aimed at improving energy efficiency and reducing the size of devices.
